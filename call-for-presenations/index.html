The importance of answering this call

We need your ideas, talents, and insights for the best possible conference!

The quality of a conference is a function of the people who present and share their ideas, tools, and content. At FOSS4G, those people are you, the incredible community that develops and uses free and open-source geospatial software.

Process

Timeline
Tuesday, March 21: Call for presentations closes
Monday, March 27: Community voting opens
Monday, April 10: Community voting closes
Monday, May 1: Accepted presentations announced

We expect to have 200–250 talks selected through this Call for Presentations. Keynote and invited presentations will round out the program. The volunteer FOSS4G Program Committee will lead the process but you, the attendees, will help select the presentations through an open Community Voting process. The most popular presentations will be included in the program, and the Program Committee will select the remainder to provide a diversity of voices and content.

We are looking for a diverse array of presentations

The Program Committee is looking for a variety of different types of stories across a broad range of sectors and industries. During the Call for Presentations registration process you will be asked to tag your abstract in the following ways:

Themes — one general area that describes your presentation:

FOSS4G Development
New and updated tools and projects: the traditional cornerstone of this conference

Drones; Internet of Things; Remote Sensing
Education
Curricula; Community
Business

Government

The Sciences
Climate and environment; Natural resources; Public health; Data science

Topics — one or more FOSS4G-focused subjects covered by your presention:

  • Adoption
  • Use cases and applications
  • Research
  • Lessons learned
